Glomerulopathy Differential & Workup
Start with the clinical presentation pattern:
Step 1
Classify Syndrome
Determine if nephrotic (protein ≥3.5g/d, edema, low albumin, hyperlipidemia) vs nephritic (hematuria, RBC casts, AKI, hypertension, mild proteinuria) vs mixed
Step 2
Nephrotic Syndrome — Differential
Minimal Change Disease
Age: children >adults · Abrupt onset · Lipoid nephrosis · Effacement on EM only
Bx: Normal LMTx: Steroids
FSGS
Primary vs secondary (obesity, HIV, heroin, reflux) · Columbia classification · Steroid resistance
Bx: Focal sclerosisTx: Steroids ± CNI
Membranous Nephropathy
PLA2R Ab in 70-80% primary · "Spike and dome" on EM · Thrombotic risk high
Bx: Thickened GBMTx: Rituximab
Diabetic Nephropathy
Most common cause ESRD · Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules · GBM thickening · Mesangial expansion
Bx: Often clinical DxTx: SGLT2i, RAAS, finerenone
Amyloidosis
AL (plasma cell) vs AA (inflammatory) · Congo red: apple-green birefringence · Waxy casts
Bx: Congo red+Tx: Treat underlying
Step 3
Nephritic Syndrome — Differential
IgA Nephropathy
Most common GN worldwide · Post-URI hematuria · Oxford MEST-C · Mesangial IgA deposits
IgA mesangialIF: IgA dominant
Lupus Nephritis
ISN/RPS Class I-VI · ANA, dsDNA, complement low · Class III/IV most aggressive
Subendothelial depositsTx: MMF or CYC
ANCA Vasculitis
GPA (c-ANCA/PR3) vs MPA (p-ANCA/MPO) · Pauci-immune · Crescents · Pulmonary-renal
Pauci-immuneTx: Rituximab ± steroids
Post-Strep GN
Children · Low C3, normal C4 · ASO titer · Hump deposits (subepithelial) on EM
Humps on EMTx: Supportive
Anti-GBM Disease
Linear IgG IF · Pulmonary-renal · Rapidly progressive GN · Goodpasture = anti-GBM + pulm hemorrhage
Linear IgGTx: Plasmapheresis + CYC
Step 4
Serologic Workup
Always order: ANA, dsDNA, ANCA (c/p), anti-GBM, complement (C3, C4, CH50), SPEP/UPEP, free light chains, anti-PLA2R, cryoglobulins, hepatitis B&C, HIV, blood cultures if febrile. Biopsy is gold standard — discuss risk/benefit with patient.
AKI Evaluation Approach
KDIGO Stage
Define AKI Severity
Stage 1: Cr ×1.5–1.9 baseline OR ↑ ≥0.3 mg/dL OR UO <0.5 mL/kg/h ×6–12h
Stage 2: Cr ×2–2.9 baseline OR UO <0.5 mL/kg/h ×12h
Stage 3: Cr ×3 OR ≥4.0 mg/dL OR RRT initiated OR UO <0.3 mL/kg/h ×24h or anuria ×12h
Localize
Pre-renal vs Intrinsic vs Post-renal
Clinical Clues
Prerenal (FeNa <1%, BUN:Cr >20) Intrinsic (casts, FENa >2%) Post-renal (US first)
Workup
Key Diagnostic Steps
Urine studies: UA + microscopy (muddy brown casts = ATN, RBC casts = GN, WBC casts = AIN/pyelonephritis), spot protein/Cr, FeNa, FeUrea (if on diuretics)
Imaging: Renal US (obstruction, echogenicity, size)
Labs: BMP, CBC, LFTs, coags, LDH, haptoglobin (TMA), SPEP, complement if GN suspected
Biopsy: Consider if unexplained intrinsic AKI, GN pattern, or AIN suspected
Management
General AKI Principles
Remove nephrotoxins · Optimize hemodynamics · Hold ACEi/ARB in acute phase · Monitor K, bicarb, fluid balance daily · Nutritional support · Avoid contrast if possible · Dose-adjust all renally-cleared meds · RRT if refractory to medical management
Hypertension Management in CKD
BP Targets
KDIGO 2021 / ACC/AHA Goals
CKD (non-dialysis): SBP <120 mmHg if tolerated (SPRINT criteria) — use standardized office BP measurement
KDIGO 2021: Target <120 mmHg SBP for most CKD patients not on dialysis
Proteinuric CKD: <130/80 preferred
HD patients: Pre-dialysis <140/90; post-dialysis <130/80
First-Line
RAAS Blockade
Proteinuria ≥300 mg/g (A2/A3): ACEi or ARB is first-line regardless of diabetes
Monitor: K+, Cr at 1–2 weeks after initiation or dose increase
Acceptable Cr rise: Up to 30% above baseline is acceptable — do NOT stop for this
STOP if: K >5.5 despite management, Cr rise >30%, bilateral RAS suspected
Add-on Agents
Step-Wise Therapy
Add in order
+ CCB (amlodipine) + Thiazide/Loop diuretic + Finerenone (if T2DM) Resistant HTN workup
Hyperkalemia Management Protocol
Assess
Severity Classification
Mild: K 5.0–5.5 mEq/L — usually asymptomatic, monitor closely
Moderate: K 5.5–6.0 mEq/L — dietary counseling, hold ACEi/ARB/K-sparing diuretics
Severe: K >6.0 mEq/L OR ECG changes — URGENT treatment
ECG changes: Peaked T → PR prolongation → Wide QRS → Sine wave → VF
Acute
Emergency Treatment (K >6.5 or ECG changes)
1. Membrane stabilization: Ca gluconate 1g IV over 2–3 min (onset 1–3 min, duration 30–60 min) — does NOT lower K
2. Transcellular shift: Regular insulin 10U IV + D50W 1 amp; Albuterol 10–20mg nebulized; NaHCO3 if acidotic (pH <7.2)
3. K removal: Furosemide IV if volume overloaded; Kayexalate/patiromer/SZC orally; Dialysis for refractory/ESRD
Chronic
Long-term Prevention
Diet: K <2–3g/day (avoid high-K foods: bananas, potatoes, tomatoes, oranges)
Medications: Patiromer (Veltassa) or SZC (Lokelma) — allow continued RAAS therapy
Review meds: TMP-SMX, heparin, NSAIDs, beta-blockers, K-sparing diuretics
RRT Initiation Criteria
Emergent
AEIOU Indications (Initiate Immediately)
Acidosis — pH <7.1 refractory to bicarbonate
Electrolytes — K >6.5 refractory to medical management
Ingestions — dialyzable toxins (lithium, ethylene glycol, salicylates, methanol)
Overload — volume overload refractory to diuretics
Uremia — encephalopathy, pericarditis, coagulopathy, BUN typically >80–100
Elective CKD
Pre-Dialysis Planning (eGFR <20–25)
KDOQI: Start education at eGFR <30; access placement at eGFR <20
Timing: IDEAL trial — early vs late start (eGFR 10–15) showed no survival difference; initiate based on symptoms
Access: AV fistula (preferred) or graft; tunneled catheter for HD if urgent; PD catheter 2–4 weeks before PD start
Modality decision: HD vs PD vs home HD — shared decision-making; discuss transplant candidacy
Modality
HD vs PD Considerations
Factors favoring each modality
PD: Home, residual function, autonomy HD: Clinic support, abdominal issues
